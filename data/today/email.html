Day 37. Clean rooms are the industry's way of saying we do not trust each other, formalized as infrastructure. The promise: two parties match their customer data inside a neutral environment, neither side walks away with the other's rows, and the output is aggregate. Overlap analysis, attribution, audience building, all without raw PII changing hands. The reality check: a clean room is only as honest as its aggregation thresholds and query controls. If you can slice an aggregate down to a cell of three users, you have built a re-identification engine with better branding. What I watch in any clean room setup: minimum cohort sizes, who controls the match key, whether the join is deterministic or modeled, and what leaves the room as activation-ready output. Those four answers tell you whether it is privacy infrastructure or theater. Used well, clean rooms are genuinely useful. Used as a compliance costume, they are the same old data exchange with extra steps and better lawyers.
