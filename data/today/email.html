Day 34. Householding is the least glamorous problem in identity, and the one that decides whether your retail media measurement is fiction. Two adults, one address, one streaming account, three devices, one shared credit card. Ship a diaper ad because the household bought diapers, and attribution works. Report that one individual saw the ad and another converted, and you have double-counted a family as an audience of strangers. The mechanics are unforgiving. Address normalization has to survive apartment numbers, USPS standardization, and people who type their own street name wrong. You need rules for roommates, for adult children, for the couple that separates but keeps the Prime account. My rule of thumb after years of this: individual identity for personalization, household identity for measurement, and a written policy for when each applies. Teams that blur the two produce metrics nobody should sign. The household is the real economic unit of retail. Model it deliberately or it will model itself badly.
