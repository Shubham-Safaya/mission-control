Today: LinkedIn Day 35

Day 35. Everyone in adtech says identity graph. Almost nobody agrees on what the nodes are. A RampID points at a pseudonymous person built from LiveRamp's offline spine. UID2 is an email or phone, hashed, rotated, and passed through the bidstream. A household LUID collapses individuals into an address-level economic unit. Retailer core IDs sit on top of first-party transactions, which is the quiet advantage: they are anchored to money actually spent. These are not interchangeable, and translation between them is where fidelity dies. Every hop across graphs sheds coverage and adds ambiguity, and the loss is rarely printed on the invoice. When someone shows me a cross-graph match rate, my first question is which direction the translation ran, because the number is usually asymmetric. Interoperability is the industry's favorite word and its least delivered feature. Plan your architecture assuming translation loss, and you will be pleasantly surprised exactly never.
