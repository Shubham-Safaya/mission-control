The interview question I ask every identity vendor and every senior candidate, because it has no memorizable answer.

Here is the setup: a customer requests deletion. Your graph links their submitted email to a phone, a device, and a household with one other adult. The other adult has not requested anything. Walk me through what you delete, what you keep, and what happens to the household entity.

There is no perfect answer, which is the point. The identifiers are individually owned but the household is jointly constructed. Delete too much and you have degraded another person's data without their request. Delete too little and you have retained the requester through their cohabitant's shadow.

Strong answers reason about ownership boundaries, tombstoning, derived-data propagation, and what the law actually requires versus what trust suggests. Weak answers recite we are fully compliant.

The households in your graph are shared documents. Deletion policy is where you discover whether anyone thought about the sharing.
