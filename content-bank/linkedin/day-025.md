What my PyPI download graph taught me about developer trust, which turns out to be the same thing enterprise trust is made of.

When I published my identity resolution engine, downloads trickled. The inflection came after I did three unglamorous things: wrote failure-mode documentation that admits where the matcher is weak, added the explainability API so every merge can say why, and published precision numbers on a synthetic benchmark anyone can reproduce.

Nothing about the algorithm changed. What changed is that a stranger could now audit my claims without trusting me. Reproducibility did the selling.

I think about this constantly in vendor-land, where the standard sales motion is the opposite: impressive numbers, undisclosed methodology, benchmarks you cannot rerun. It works until a buyer builds their own truth set, and then it stops working all at once.

Open source taught me the durable sales pitch is an audit trail. Enterprises are just developers with procurement departments.
