Nobody budgets for identity QA, so let me put numbers on what it costs when skipped.

A false merge that leaks one customer's order history to another is a support escalation, a possible regulatory notification, and in a bad case a headline. Multiply by however many silent months it ran.

A stale suppression list that keeps marketing to someone who opted out is statutory exposure per message in several jurisdictions. The arithmetic gets uncomfortable at email scale.

A household over-merge contaminating your measurement control groups quietly inflates campaign lift, which reallocates real budget toward channels that did not earn it. That one never surfaces as an incident. It surfaces as years of slightly wrong strategy.

Against all this, identity QA costs a sampled human review queue, a truth set refreshed quarterly, and anomaly alarms on merge behavior. A rounding error against one incident.

Unglamorous insurance is still insurance. Buy it before the actuary shows up in the form of an audit.
