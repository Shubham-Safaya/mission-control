The org chart problem in identity: the graph serves everyone, so it reports to no one.

Marketing funds it when campaigns need audiences. Engineering owns the pipelines but not the policy. Legal owns the risk but not the roadmap. Analytics depends on it silently and files tickets when entity counts jump. Each function optimizes its slice, and the graph as a whole optimizes for whoever shouted most recently.

The failure is structural, so the fix is structural: identity needs a named product owner with a mandate that spans the consumers, an error budget reviewed like uptime, and a roadmap that trades off marketing recall against privacy precision explicitly, in writing, with sign-off.

I have watched this role not exist at companies with nine-figure data budgets, and I have watched one senior appointment quietly fix incidents that three reorgs could not.

If everyone owns the graph, entropy owns the graph. Name the owner. The title matters less than the mandate.
