The two clocks problem in customer data, which every architecture eventually meets.

Marketing runs on the campaign clock: hours and days, launch windows, real-time triggers. Identity truth runs on the life clock: people move annually, change emails rarely, form households slowly. The graph updates on the second clock while being queried on the first, and the gap between clocks is where stale targeting lives.

You cannot make the life clock faster. You can make the system honest about the gap: every identity assertion carries a last-corroborated timestamp, and activation logic degrades gracefully with staleness. Fresh identities get precision treatment. Aging ones fall back to broader, safer logic. Ancient ones get suppressed from anything sensitive.

Most systems instead present every assertion as equally current, which is how a customer who moved in March receives geo-targeted offers for their old city in November.

Time is an identity attribute. Model it, or it will model your errors.
