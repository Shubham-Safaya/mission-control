A thing I wish someone had told me before my first vendor data integration: third-party data does not fail loudly. It fails politely.

The file lands on schedule. The schema validates. The match rate holds. And quietly, the underlying panel that feeds the segments you buy has aged, shifted, or lost a source, and the income deciles you activate against are eighteen months stale.

Vendors rarely volunteer refresh lineage because nobody asks. So I ask, in writing, per field: what feeds this attribute, when was the feed last replaced, what fraction of records carry observed versus modeled values, and what changed in the last four quarters.

The modeled-versus-observed question is the one that matters most. Modeled income on top of modeled age on top of a probabilistic person is a probability sandwich sold as a demographic.

Buy third-party data the way you would buy produce. Ask when it was picked, not just whether the box looks nice.
