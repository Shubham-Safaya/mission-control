What a decade of vendor QBRs taught me to listen for, beneath the slides.

The healthy signals are mundane. They bring the same metric definitions as last quarter, so trends are real. They report a failure you did not catch, unprompted, with the fix. Their roadmap has items your team requested, dated, and one they declined, reasoned. The account team includes someone who can answer a technical question without a follow-up email.

The warning signs are equally mundane. Metric definitions drift quarter to quarter, always favorably. Every number is a record high in a market you know is flat. Questions about precision get answered with roadmap. And the phrase we are becoming a platform appears near a price increase.

None of this is dramatic. Vendor decay rarely is: it is three consecutive quarters of vocabulary doing the work numbers used to do.

Keep last year's deck in the meeting folder. Continuity is the cheapest audit there is.
