The loyalty program is the most underrated identity asset in retail, and it was never really about points.

Look at what a loyalty signup actually purchases. A verified email or phone, sometimes both. A durable customer key that survives device churn, browser resets, and cookie deprecation, because the customer carries it willingly. Consent, collected with actual consideration in return. And an incentive for the customer to identify themselves at checkout, both online and in-store, which stitches the hardest join in retail: offline transactions to digital identity.

The points cost real margin. What they buy is a self-healing, consented identity spine that no vendor can license to your competitor.

This is why retail media and loyalty programs grew up together. The media network monetizes exactly the asset the loyalty program maintains.

If you want to value a retailer's media business, do not start with impressions. Start with what fraction of transactions carry a loyalty identifier.
