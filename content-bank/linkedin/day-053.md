On resisting the temptation to over-model: sometimes the best identity feature is a question in the checkout flow.

Teams spend quarters inferring household composition from purchase patterns, device overlap, and address history. Meanwhile the loyalty signup asks nothing beyond email, because every added field costs conversion.

But declared data beats inferred data on every axis that matters: precision, consent clarity, durability, and audit defensibility. One optional question, answered by even a fifth of customers, out-signals a model trained on everyone.

The craft is making declaration worth it. Household size in exchange for family-sized offers is a fair trade customers actually take. Birthday for a birthday reward converts absurdly well. Each declared field then becomes truth data that calibrates the inference for everyone who stayed silent.

Infer what you must. Ask what you can. The model should be the fallback, not the strategy. The checkout flow is an identity instrument. Treat it like one.
