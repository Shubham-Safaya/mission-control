Signal loss is not coming. It arrived years ago, and the industry is still negotiating with the corpse of the third-party cookie.

Cookie deprecation headlines rotated between delayed and dead so many times that people stopped tracking the actual scoreboard. Safari and Firefox blocked third-party cookies long ago. Mobile identifiers went opt-in. Chrome wobbled publicly for years. Whatever the final policy news says, the strategic answer stopped changing in 2020.

The durable spine is authenticated first-party identity. An email or phone a customer gave you on purpose, with consent recorded, refreshed by a real relationship. Everything else is decoration around that fact.

This is why retailers and anyone with login-worthy value quietly became the new oligarchs of adtech. Identity used to be inferred from surveillance. Increasingly it is granted through trust, and trust does not deprecate on a browser team's schedule.

Build for the world where the customer has to want to be known. It is already here.
