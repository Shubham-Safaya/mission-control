How I evaluate identity vendors, after sitting through more of these pitches than I care to count.

I ask for the failure modes before the success stories. A vendor who can articulate where their graph is weak, which geographies, which age bands, which signal types, has actually measured it. A vendor who says it just works has measured their sales funnel.

I ask what happens to their coverage when a specific signal source dries up, because one always does. The answer reveals whether their spine is diversified or one licensing deal in a trench coat.

I ask for precision at a stated threshold on a truth set I supply, not theirs. Synthetic populations are wonderful for this: I know the ground truth because I manufactured it.

And I ask who else touches the data, because fourth parties are where privacy programs go to be surprised.

Vendor evaluation is not procurement. It is due diligence on someone else's decade of shortcuts.
