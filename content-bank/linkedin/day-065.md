On the difference between a data moat and a data pile, which the market keeps confusing.

A pile is big: records accumulated, licensed, appended. Its value decays with staleness, its liabilities compound with regulation, and competitors can assemble equivalent piles from the same vendors.

A moat regenerates. A retailer's transaction stream, an authenticated platform's login graph, a loyalty program's daily identifier refresh, these produce identity continuously as a byproduct of value customers actually want. Nobody can license their way to an equivalent, because the moat is the relationship, not the records.

The diligence question that separates them: what happens to this dataset if acquisition stops for a year? Piles decay toward worthlessness. Moats barely notice, because the flywheel is the operating business itself.

Every identity partnership pitch should be read through this lens. You are either renting access to someone's flywheel, or buying a photograph of last year's pile. The invoice looks identical. The five-year outcome does not.
