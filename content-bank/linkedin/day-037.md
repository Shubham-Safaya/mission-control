The most useful mental model I have for CDP shopping: you are not buying software, you are choosing which failure you can live with.

Suite CDPs fail closed. Everything integrates, nothing is best-in-class, and when the identity resolution disagrees with reality, your recourse is a support ticket and a roadmap slide.

Composable stacks fail open. Warehouse-native, every component swappable, resolution logic you own, and the integration burden lives with your team forever. When it breaks, the recourse is your own engineers, which is either comforting or terrifying depending on your headcount.

Neither failure mode is wrong. What is wrong is choosing based on the demo instead of the failure. The demo shows the happy path, and identity systems are defined by their unhappy paths.

My forcing question for any architecture review: walk me through the morning after a bad merge event in this design. Who notices, who can act, who owns the fix. The stack that answers cleanly wins.
