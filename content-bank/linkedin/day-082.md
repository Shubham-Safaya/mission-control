What loyalty fraud taught me about identity, from the strangest quarter of my learning.

Points programs attract creative adversaries: multi-accounting for signup bonuses, receipt recycling, household identity abuse where one power user farms the family. Investigating these patterns is an involuntary masterclass in your own graph's blind spots, because fraudsters find the seams professionally.

Every fraud pattern is an identity assertion your system believed too cheaply. The lesson generalizes far beyond fraud: the same seams that admit adversaries admit ordinary error. The shared-device household that lets a points farmer operate also confuses your frequency capping. The email-plus-tag trick that spawns bonus accounts also fragments your LTV model.

I now think fraud review belongs in identity QA, not just in risk. Adversaries are unpaid penetration testers of your entity resolution, and their findings ship weekly whether you read them or not.

Study whoever games your graph. They audited it before you did.
