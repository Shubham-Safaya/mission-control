Advice I give mid-career PMs who feel behind in the AI cycle while working in data infrastructure.

The models everyone is racing to ship all share a dependency nobody tweets about: resolved, trustworthy entities. Recommendation quality is capped by identity quality. Personalization built on fragmented profiles personalizes for fragments. LLM agents acting on customer context inherit whatever entity mess sits underneath, at higher speed and confidence.

Which means the boring specialization is quietly appreciating. Every advance in the layer above raises the cost of errors in the layer below, and the people who can operate that lower layer, measure it, and explain it to regulators do not multiply the way prompt-writing skill does.

You do not need to be at the frontier to compound from it. You need to own a dependency of the frontier.

Infrastructure careers work like infrastructure: ignored in booms, indispensable always. Choose your layer and be patient with it.
