The single most clarifying exercise I have done as a data PM: computing the revenue-per-resolved-identity of the systems I work near.

Take any data-driven revenue line: media, personalization lift, retention. Divide by the count of genuinely resolved, reachable identities that powered it. The quotient reframes every architecture debate.

Suddenly the case for fixing householding is not hygiene, it is a specific dollar figure times the merge errors contaminating measurement. The case for loyalty investment is the delta between an identified and anonymous transaction, multiplied by billions of them. The case against buying yet another enrichment feed is that it adds attributes to identities that were never reachable anyway.

Most identity work is sold internally as infrastructure, which is why it loses budget fights to features. Denominated per identity, it becomes a P&L conversation.

If you cannot compute this number for your own stack, that fact is itself the finding. It means identity value is nobody's number, which means it is nobody's job.
