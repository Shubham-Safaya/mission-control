An unpopular opinion about personalization: most of it is inventory management wearing a psychology costume.

The industry narrative says personalization works because messages resonate with individual identity. The operational reality inside most programs: personalization works mostly by suppressing waste. Not showing dog food to cat owners. Not retargeting the thing bought yesterday. Not emailing the person who unsubscribed in spirit but not in checkbox.

Suppression is identity-hungry in exactly the ways this series keeps describing: it needs the purchase joined to the person, the household deduplicated, the opt-out resolved across channels. Get that right and the lift arrives before a single clever message is written, because you stopped paying to annoy people.

The sequencing implication is unfashionable: fund negative personalization first. It is cheaper, safer, measurable, and it finances the romantic kind.

Relevance begins with restraint. The algorithm that knows when to stay silent is the one making money.
