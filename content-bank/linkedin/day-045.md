The metric I trust most when auditing an identity system is one almost nobody dashboards: split-merge churn.

Count how many identities merged last month. Count how many split. A healthy graph shows modest, explainable movement: people genuinely change emails, households genuinely form and dissolve. An unhealthy graph oscillates, merging thousands of pairs one month and splitting them back the next as thresholds wobble or a normalizer flips behavior between releases.

Oscillation is worse than either error alone, because every flip whipsaws downstream systems. The frequency cap resets. The suppression list misses. The LTV model watches a customer teleport between existence states.

Match rate stays flat through all of this, which is exactly why match rate is the metric executives see. Churn is where the instability shows.

One graph metric to add this quarter: identity half-life, the median time a resolved identity survives unchanged. If it is shorter than your campaign cycle, your audiences are dissolving mid-flight.
