The penultimate post of this series, on the question I get asked most: is identity resolution a solved problem now, with modern ML?

The matching arithmetic was never the hard part, and better embeddings make an already-adequate part better. What remains unsolved is everything around the arithmetic. Ground truth stays expensive, so precision stays estimated. Populations keep churning, so graphs keep decaying. Consent keeps evolving, so yesterday's legal spine keeps needing re-audits. Organizations keep fragmenting ownership, so entropy keeps winning quarters. And every improvement in matching power raises the stakes of matching errors, because confident systems get trusted with more.

Solved implies a destination. Identity is maintenance: of accuracy against churn, of permissions against time, of trust against entropy. The tooling improves. The nature of the work does not.

Anyone selling you the end of this problem is selling you the beginning of a different one, renamed.
