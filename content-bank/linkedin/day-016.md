Non-endemic advertising is retail media's second act, and it is a bigger identity test than the first.

Act one: sell diaper ads to diaper brands against diaper purchases. The measurement loop is closed and nobody argues about relevance.

Act two: sell insurance, streaming, autos, and travel against the same logged-in audience. The retailer's data leaves its natural habitat. Now the pitch is not we know they buy your product. It is we know who they are, at scale, with income and life-stage signals inferred from baskets.

This is precisely where identity precision becomes a brand-safety issue for the retailer itself. Serve a car ad on a fiction, nothing happens. Report to a national insurer that your audience was thirty-five to fifty-four homeowners when your householding is mush, and you burn credibility with money that has options.

Non-endemic revenue is a bet that your graph is as good as your category data. Some retailers are about to learn the difference publicly.
