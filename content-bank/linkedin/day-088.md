What I would tell a new grad joining a customer data team this year, in five sentences I wish I had received.

Learn the failure modes before the features: every system you will touch is defined by how it breaks, and the breaks are not in the onboarding deck. Sit with customer support for a week early; the graph's errors surface there first, translated into human frustration. Write everything you learn in public form, even if it never leaves the company wiki, because compression is comprehension. Treat every threshold, weight, and window you encounter as a decision someone made under constraints, and go find out what the constraints were. And keep one artifact you built end-to-end yourself, however small, because in this field the people who are trusted are the people who can be inspected.

The domain will change vocabularies every three years. These will not.
