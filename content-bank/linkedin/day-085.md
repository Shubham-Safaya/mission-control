A field observation on data partnerships: the contract negotiates the happy path, the integration reveals the truth.

Every data deal I have watched closely had the same shape. Months on commercial terms, exclusivity, pricing tiers. Then integration begins, and the actual discoveries arrive: their refresh cadence is monthly where the deck implied weekly, their identifier coverage skews regionally in ways that reshape the value, their deletion propagation SLA exists in spirit only.

None of this was hidden maliciously. It simply lives below the altitude where deals are negotiated, in the layer where files have schemas and schemas have surprises.

The fix I now insist on: a technical pilot clause, before commercial lock-in, with exit rights tied to measured findings. Two weeks of real files through real pipes surfaces what two hundred pages of contract cannot.

Sign the pilot with enthusiasm and the contract with evidence. The order matters more than the terms.
