Why I stopped saying data-driven and started saying evidence-weighted, and what changed.

Data-driven, as practiced, often means a number was present when the decision happened. The number may have been unvalidated, the metric misdefined, the causality imagined, but the ritual was observed and the decision proceeded with borrowed authority.

Evidence-weighted asks more: how was this number produced, what would make it wrong, how heavily should it count against judgment, anecdote, and prior? Sometimes the honest weight of the data is low, and the experienced operator's pattern-match deserves the tiebreak. Saying so out loud is not a betrayal of analytics. It is analytics.

In identity work this distinction is existential, because our numbers look precise while resting on probabilistic foundations. A match rate to two decimal places, built on thresholds nobody has recalibrated since 2023, deserves limited weight, and pretending otherwise is numerology.

Respect data enough to interrogate it. Reverence is not respect.
