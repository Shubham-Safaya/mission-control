The measurement number I would ban from executive dashboards if I could ban exactly one: blended ROAS.

Blended ROAS averages the return across channels, audiences, and campaign types into a single confident decimal. It goes up when you shift budget toward bottom-funnel branded audiences who were buying anyway. It goes down when you invest in the incremental customers who are the actual point of advertising. It rewards precisely the wrong instinct, quarter after quarter, with the authority of a KPI.

The identity connection: blending is only necessary because channel-level attribution is contested, and channel-level attribution is contested because cross-channel identity is hard. The blur at the identity layer becomes strategic blindness at the boardroom layer.

The replacement is not one number. It is incrementality by audience tier, measured with holdouts, reported with intervals. Less satisfying. More true.

Any metric that cannot go down when you make a bad decision is not a metric. It is a lullaby.
