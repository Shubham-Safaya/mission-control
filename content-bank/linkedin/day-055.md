A pattern from mentoring PMs trying to break into data platform roles: the portfolio gap is almost never technical. It is evidentiary.

Candidates tell me they understand identity resolution, and they do, conversationally. What they cannot do is point at anything. No repo, no writeup, no demo, no artifact where a hiring panel can watch their thinking operate.

The fix does not require employer data or permission. Public aggregate data and synthetic populations are enough to demonstrate every mechanism this field runs on: build a matcher, publish its precision curve on manufactured truth, write honestly about where it breaks. That artifact outperforms every bullet point on a resume, because it is the job, observable.

I know this works because it is what I did, and the compound interest on public proof-of-work keeps surprising me.

The barrier to evidence has never been lower. The differentiator is deciding your understanding should be inspectable.
