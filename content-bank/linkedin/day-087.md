The migration nobody plans for: leaving your own legacy identity system.

Teams plan vendor exits. Almost nobody plans exits from their own historical decisions: the internal graph built in 2019, the customer keys minted by a system nobody dares turn off, the merge history that downstream systems silently depend on.

The trap is that internal systems have no contract end date to force the conversation, so they persist by default while the world moves. The customer key that seemed permanent becomes a translation liability. The merge decisions made under old thresholds become inherited, unexplainable precedent.

What planning looks like: key versioning from day one, so identity references carry their era. Translation tables as first-class, maintained artifacts. And a standing rule that any system consuming identity must tolerate re-resolution, because re-resolution is not an incident, it is a season.

Your legacy system was once someone's clean-slate design. Build today's clean slate with its own retirement in the requirements.
