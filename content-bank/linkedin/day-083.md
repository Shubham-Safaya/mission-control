On writing the second draft of an identity strategy after the first one met reality.

Draft one, written before scale: one graph to rule them all, real-time everything, universal identifiers, a single golden threshold. Elegant on the whiteboard. The whiteboard does not have customers.

Draft two, written after: multiple confidence views over one substrate, batch where batch is honest, identifier portfolios instead of a universal key, and thresholds owned per use case by the teams who bear their errors. Less elegant. Dramatically more operable.

The interesting part is what survived both drafts unchanged: explainability as a hard requirement, consent as a first-class field, and the sampled human review queue. The load-bearing ideas turned out to be the governance ones, not the architectural ones.

Strategies are hypotheses wearing formal clothing. The second draft is where the actual strategy lives, and getting there faster is the only speed that matters.
