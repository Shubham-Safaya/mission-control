The activation spec is the most revealing document in adtech, and almost nobody outside the integration team ever reads one.

When an audience travels from a brand to a platform, it travels as a payload: segment definition, identifier type, match instructions, refresh cadence, suppression rules. Reading that payload tells you the truth the campaign deck abstracts away.

You learn which identifier actually carries the audience, and it is often a coarser one than the pitch implied. You learn the refresh cadence, and discover the precision audience updates monthly while the campaign optimizes hourly. You learn what happens to non-matches, which is usually silence.

I built a public demo where segment building outputs exactly this payload against synthetic people, because I think practitioners should see the shape of the thing. The interface between planning and delivery is where strategies go to be simplified.

Ask to read the spec on your next big activation. The document is short. The education is not.
