How signal loss changed vendor due diligence, in one before-and-after.

Before: the coverage question. How many identifiers do you hold, how fresh, matched how. The graph was the product, and graphs could be compared on size.

After: the provenance question. For every identifier in your spine, what is the consent story, and which browser, platform, or statute can delete it overnight. Size still matters, but survivability prices the deal.

I have watched impressive graphs lose double-digit coverage from a single policy change upstream, because their spine leaned on one signal source everyone pretended was diversified. The vendors that endure are the ones whose identifiers customers deliberately gave: authenticated relationships, loyalty data, consented enrichment.

The new diligence question I always ask: show me your coverage by consent class, and tell me which class grew last year. Growth in the durable class is the only growth that compounds.

Buy graphs the way you would buy bonds. Check what backs them.
