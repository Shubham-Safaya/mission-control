A short field guide to reading identity vendor case studies, developed over too many of them.

The customer is unnamed: the results are unverifiable by design. Match rate appears without a precision partner: you are reading the recall half of a two-number story. Uplift is quoted against a baseline that is not described: the baseline was chosen after the result. The words directionally or modeled appear near the money number: the money number is soft. Scale is quoted in identifiers rather than people: divide by three to seven.

None of this means the vendor is bad. It means case studies are marketing, and identity marketing has structural incentives toward the flattering numerator.

The countermove never changes: insist on a pilot where you hold ground truth. A synthetic population works. A holdout of your own CRM works better. Two weeks of pilot beats two hundred pages of case studies.

Trust arithmetic you can rerun. Nothing else survives procurement.
