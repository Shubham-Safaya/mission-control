The consent record is becoming the most valuable field in your customer database, and most schemas treat it as a boolean.

Consented: true. To what? Collected when, under which policy version, through which surface, for which purposes, revocable how? A boolean cannot answer a regulator, cannot survive a purpose-limitation audit, and cannot express the difference between someone who opted into everything at signup and someone who grudgingly allowed order updates.

The teams doing this well model consent as an event stream, not a flag. Every grant, every revocation, every policy version, timestamped and attached to the resolved identity, not just the raw identifier. That last part is the identity dependency again: consent given under one email must govern the person, or your graph launders permissions.

Signal loss made first-party data the asset. Consent architecture decides whether that asset is actually usable. The retailers that treated consent as product surface rather than legal furniture are now sitting on the only audiences that are both reachable and defensible.
