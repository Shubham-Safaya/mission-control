Two-thirds through this series, a note on what writing daily about identity has done to my actual work.

Forcing one idea into three hundred words every weekday turns out to be an engineering discipline. Vague understandings do not survive the format. Either I can explain why blocking keys cap recall in plain sentences, or I discover I only half-knew it and the post becomes the education.

The unexpected compounding: colleagues now arrive at meetings having read the thinking, so discussions start at decision rather than definition. Vendors quote my own posts back to me, which is surreal and clarifying, because it reveals which framings actually travel.

And the archive has become a hiring signal I never planned: candidates reference specific posts in interviews, which shows me instantly who reads deeply in this space.

Write publicly about your niche. Not for the audience metrics. For the compression it forces on your own thinking, which is worth more than the followers.
