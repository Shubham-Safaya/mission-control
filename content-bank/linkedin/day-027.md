Signal loss made measurement honest in one specific way nobody celebrates: it killed the fiction that we were ever measuring individuals on the open web.

The cookie-era claim was person-level attribution across sites. What we actually had was device-level guessing with a confident interface. When the identifiers dried up, the industry did not lose person-level truth. It lost the ability to pretend.

What replaces it splits by trust level. Inside authenticated environments, real person and household measurement, better than anything the cookie ever delivered. Outside them, aggregate methods: geo experiments, marketing mix models, clean-room cohorts with minimum sizes. Both are more honest than what they replaced.

The teams struggling most right now are the ones trying to rebuild the old fiction with new parts. The teams winning accepted the split: precision where identity is real, statistics where it is not, and no pretending at the boundary.

Honesty turned out to be a viable measurement strategy. Who knew.
