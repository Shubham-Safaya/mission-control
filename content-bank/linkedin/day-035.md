Building in public taught me something about data products specifically: the demo is the documentation.

For months I described my identity engine in words. Normalization, blocking, fuzzy matching, graph assembly. Polite interest. Then I built a synthetic population demo where you can inspect an actual household, see its records, and watch which rule linked them. Same engine, zero new capability. Completely different reception.

Identity work is invisible almost by definition. Success means nothing weird happened. So the burden is on us to make the machinery observable: show the match that fired, the score it earned, the household it built.

I now believe every data platform team should maintain one living, clickable demonstration of their core mechanism on safe data. Not for customers. For the twelve internal meetings a year where someone with budget authority asks what the team actually does.

Show the graph doing its job. Abstractions do not survive reorgs. Demos do.
