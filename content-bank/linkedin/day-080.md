The last mile of trust in data products is always a human being who decided to be accountable.

Systems assert. Dashboards display. Vendors certify. And at some point a specific person tells a specific executive that the audience count is real, the deletion completed, the measurement holds, and stakes their credibility on it. Every mature data organization I have seen runs on a small number of these people, and every immature one runs on the assumption that the tooling makes them unnecessary.

The tooling does not. Tools produce claims. Accountability converts claims into decisions others can safely build on, and it cannot be automated because it is not information. It is skin in the game.

This is my quiet criterion for hiring and for vendors alike: find the person willing to put their name on the number. Where no such person exists, the number is decoration regardless of the pipeline behind it.

Graphs link records. People underwrite them.
