Incrementality is the only advertising question that matters, and it is the one retail media answers least often.

ROAS says: people who saw the ad bought things. Incrementality asks: would they have bought anyway. For a retailer's own shoppers, targeted precisely because they already buy, the honest answer is often yes.

That is the uncomfortable heart of retail media measurement. The tighter your targeting on known purchasers, the better your ROAS looks and the less your advertising did. You are paying to take credit for gravity.

Real answers need holdouts: matched audiences where a control group sees nothing, and the delta is the ad's actual work. Retailers with strong identity spines can run this well precisely because they can build clean, household-deduplicated control groups. Household dedup matters here more than anywhere. Contaminated controls flatter everyone.

If a measurement pitch cannot describe its control group in one sentence, it is a ROAS machine. Enjoy the deck, protect the budget.
