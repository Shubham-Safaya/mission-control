Why I tell every retailer to treat their identity graph like a balance sheet item, not a marketing tool.

Marketing tools get evaluated on campaign lift. Balance sheet items get audited, depreciated, and maintained, with named owners and known valuation methods. The graph deserves the second treatment, because that is how it behaves economically.

It appreciates with loyalty penetration and consent quality. It depreciates with population churn, roughly and predictably, which means maintenance is not optional spend, it is offsetting depreciation. It carries liabilities: every stale consent record and every over-merged household is a contingent cost waiting for a regulator or an advertiser audit to realize it.

And like any asset, it can be marked to market. An advertiser running an independent match test against your claimed audience is doing exactly that.

CFOs understand this framing instantly. It is the marketing organizations that resist it, because assets come with accountability that tools do not.
