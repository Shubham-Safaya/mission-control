Retail media is eating advertising because it solved the one problem the open web never could: knowing what actually sold.

The mechanics are simple to state. A retailer has first-party transactions, a logged-in audience, and shelf space that is now also ad space. An advertiser gets closed-loop measurement: the same identity spine that served the impression also recorded the purchase.

What gets underestimated is how much of this rests on identity plumbing. Attribution needs the ad exposure and the receipt to resolve to the same person or household. Every weakness in that resolution becomes a lie in the ROAS number, and ROAS numbers move real budgets.

That is why I keep saying retail media is an identity business wearing an advertising costume. The media is commodity. The graph underneath is the product.

The retailers that win the next five years will not be the ones with the most impressions. They will be the ones whose measurement survives an auditor.
