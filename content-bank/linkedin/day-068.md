What the best data engineering partner I ever worked with taught me about PM humility.

I brought him a matching requirement stated as business logic: link records that clearly belong to the same customer. He asked me to define clearly as a number, and I could not, so he built me a distribution instead: here is what the data looks like at every similarity score, go find where your comfort ends.

That inversion changed how I write requirements. Instead of pretending certainty I did not have, I started specifying error budgets: no more than X false merges per hundred thousand, and here is a labeled truth set to measure against. The engineers stopped guessing what I meant. I stopped discovering my own assumptions in production.

Requirements are a confession of what you actually know. The best engineering partners force the confession early, and the discomfort is the value.

Bring distributions to people who think in distributions. Bring budgets to everyone else.
