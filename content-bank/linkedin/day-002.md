Match rate is the vanity metric of identity resolution, and I say that as someone whose job depends on it.

Here is the trick vendors do not put on slide one. You can push match rate up by loosening thresholds, and every point of recall you buy costs you precision somewhere you cannot see. The result is a graph that connects more records and describes fewer people.

The question I ask in every vendor evaluation is not what is your match rate. It is: against what truth set, at what precision, measured how recently, on whose population. Four qualifiers, and most pitches survive none of them.

On synthetic data where I control the ground truth, I have watched an eighty-plus percent match rate decompose into something far less flattering once you count false merges. The merge you should fear most is the confident one.

High match rate with unknown precision is not performance. It is exposure.
