Why the best identity documentation I ever inherited was written like a legal brief, and what it changed.

Most technical docs describe what the system does. This one argued: every matching rule came with its rationale, the alternatives considered, the error tradeoff accepted, the date, and the name of who accepted it. Reading it was like reading case law for the graph.

The operational difference was enormous. When a rule misfired years later, we knew whether the world had changed or the original reasoning had been wrong, which are different fixes. When legal asked why two records linked, the answer was a citation, not an archaeology project. When new engineers proposed simplifications, the doc already contained the reason the simple version had failed in 2022.

Identity systems are accumulations of judgment. Documentation that records only mechanisms throws away the judgment, which is the expensive part.

Write down the why with the what. The system will outlive everyone's memory of the difference.
