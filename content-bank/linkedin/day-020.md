Retail media network economics, explained with the only number that matters: margin on money already spent.

A retailer running national scale has already paid for the stores, the app, the loyalty program, and the checkout. The marginal cost of showing an ad to that logged-in shopper approaches software. The margin on that ad revenue makes grocery margin look like a rounding error.

That is the whole story of why every retailer with meaningful traffic suddenly has a media network, why agencies grumble about a hundred fragmented buying platforms, and why measurement standards became a battleground: nobody with eighty percent margins volunteers for commoditizing comparability.

The identity angle is the moat. Anyone can bolt on an ad server. The defensible asset is a clean, consented, transaction-anchored identity spine that measurement can stand on.

Fragmentation will consolidate. When it does, the survivors will be chosen by graph quality, not impression volume. Place your career bets accordingly.
