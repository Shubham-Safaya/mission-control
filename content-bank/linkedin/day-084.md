The quiet economics of explainability, for anyone who thinks it is a compliance tax.

Making every merge explainable cost my open-source engine perhaps fifteen percent more engineering: decision logging, rule attribution, score persistence. Here is what that fifteen percent buys, priced honestly.

Debugging drops from archaeology to lookup: incident time shrinks by an order of magnitude when the graph can say why. Vendor negotiations shift when you can produce merge-level evidence instead of aggregate complaints. Regulatory responses become exports rather than projects. And model improvements accelerate, because every labeled error comes pre-attributed to the rule that made it.

The unpriced benefit is cultural. Teams that can see why the system decided argue about evidence instead of vibes, and that changes the quality of every meeting downstream.

Explainability is not the cost of doing identity in regulated markets. It is the discount rate on every future problem. Pay it at design time, when it is cheapest.
