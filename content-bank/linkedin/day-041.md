Public data is the most underused asset in audience strategy, and I say that while working in an industry that licenses data for nine figures.

The Census Bureau publishes age, income, household structure, education, and tenure down to fine geography, free, documented, refreshed on a known cadence. FRED hands you the economic pulse of the country daily. Between them you can build a planning layer that answers where does my audience live and how is their wallet doing without a single licensed record.

It will not activate a campaign. Aggregates cannot be targeted, and that is precisely why they are safe to build on publicly.

I built a public demo on exactly this stack: Census aggregates for planning, a fully synthetic population for identity mechanics. Everything a commercial identity company demonstrates, minus the licensed PII that is their actual moat.

Knowing what the free layer can do is how you price what the paid layer is really selling. Most buyers have never established the baseline.
