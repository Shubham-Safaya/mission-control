A pattern I keep seeing in identity system postmortems: the graph did not fail. The feedback loop was never built.

Match logic ships, dashboards show match rate, everyone moves on. Six months later a merge disaster surfaces through customer support, of all places, because two strangers are seeing each other's order history. The graph had been quietly wrong for months. Nothing was watching.

Identity systems need the same operational discipline we give payments. Merge-rate anomaly alerts, because a sudden spike in merges usually means a normalizer broke, not that customers got friendlier. Sampled human review of high-impact merges. A split queue, because unmerging is harder than merging and needs first-class tooling. And a feedback channel from support tickets into graph QA.

The uncomfortable truth is that identity errors do not announce themselves in aggregate metrics. They announce themselves one furious customer at a time.

Build the loop before you need it. You will need it.
