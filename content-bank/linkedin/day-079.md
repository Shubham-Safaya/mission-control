A short note on professional patience, prompted by watching identity infrastructure finally get its moment.

The work this series describes was deeply unfashionable for most of my time in it. Budgets flowed to visible surfaces: apps, campaigns, dashboards. Identity was the plumbing you funded after an incident and forgot after the retro.

Then privacy law made the graph a compliance surface. Signal loss made first-party identity the scarce asset. Retail media made it a revenue engine. AI made entity quality a model-performance ceiling. Four macro trends, none of which the plumbing teams caused, converged to reprice a decade of unfashionable work.

I do not think I was strategic about this. I stayed because the problems were genuinely hard and the field was quiet enough to think in. The repricing was luck arriving to the prepared, which is most of what careers are.

If your specialty is unfashionable and load-bearing, hold. Fashion is cyclical. Load-bearing is forever.
