Retail media measurement has a time-zone problem nobody puts in the case studies: the attribution window is a fiction with a dropdown.

Fourteen-day click, thirty-day view, last-touch, any-touch. Move the dropdown and the same campaign swings from hero to write-off. Everyone in the room knows this, and the window still gets chosen by whoever benefits from the deck.

The identity angle is that windows exist partly to paper over resolution uncertainty. If you were sure the exposure and the purchase belonged to the same household, the honest window is however long consideration actually takes for that category. Toothpaste is not a truck.

What I would fund: category-specific windows derived from observed purchase-cycle data, published, and locked before the campaign runs. It converts measurement from negotiation to instrumentation.

Until then, the dropdown is where the truth leaks out. When someone shows me ROAS, my first question is who chose the window and when.
