The India signal: anyone building global identity strategy who ignores what is happening there is reading last decade's map.

India runs the largest real-time payment system on earth, a national biometric identity layer, and a consent-first data protection law younger and in some ways cleaner than GDPR. The DPDP Act arrived after the industry learned what compliance costs, and its consent-manager architecture is genuinely novel: consent as infrastructure, not paperwork.

For identity practitioners the interesting inversion is that mobile number, not email, is the primary key of the entire ecosystem. Commerce, banking, and messaging all resolve to the phone. Whole categories of Western identity plumbing simply do not apply, and new ones do.

I watch it closely for personal reasons, but the professional reason stands alone: when a market of that scale rebuilds identity around consented mobile-first rails, it is running the experiment the West keeps postponing.

The next identity playbook may well be written in that ecosystem first.
