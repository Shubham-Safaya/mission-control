Match rate math that every PM in this space should be able to do on a napkin.

Say you onboard one million customer records to an activation partner and they report a seventy percent match. Sounds like seven hundred thousand reachable people. It is not.

First subtract graph decay: some fraction of those matches point at identifiers that have not been seen alive in months. Then subtract channel availability: matched is not the same as biddable on the channel you actually bought. Then subtract frequency reality: reachable once is not reachable enough to matter.

By the time you reach people who will genuinely see enough impressions to move, the honest number can be less than half the headline. Your effective CPM just doubled and nobody sent a revised invoice.

I now model every activation with three numbers: claimed match, addressable match, and effectively-served audience. Budget against the third one. The first one is for the deck.
