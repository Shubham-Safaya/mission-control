The audience overlap report is due for retirement, and I want to explain why before the next one gets forwarded to me.

Two partners run a match and announce sixty percent overlap. The number feels informative. It is almost perfectly ambiguous. Overlap of which identifier, at what precision, deduplicated by whose rules, denominated on whose base? Change any of those and the same two datasets produce overlaps from thirty to eighty percent, all technically defensible.

What the number is usually used for is worse: implying audience quality, when overlap measures only intersection, not value. A high overlap with a low-quality file is high confirmation of nothing.

The upgrade costs one more column: overlap by consent class and identifier strength, with the match methodology stated on the page. Suddenly the number can support a decision.

Rule of thumb: any statistic that cannot be wrong is not information. Overlap without methodology cannot be wrong. That is the problem.
