The case for building your own benchmark, even when the industry offers you several.

Industry benchmarks answer the average question: how do systems perform on generic populations. Your business is not generic. Your customer file has its own demographic mix, its own data quality history, its own duplicate patterns from that 2019 migration nobody documents.

A private benchmark is cheap to build once you commit: sample your real distributions, generate a synthetic population that mirrors them, plant known duplicates and known distinct-but-similar pairs, and keep the answer key. Every vendor pilot, every threshold change, every quarterly regression run now grades against your reality rather than a brochure's.

Mine caught a vendor whose national numbers were excellent but whose matcher degraded sharply on multigenerational households, which my file is full of. The brochure was true. It was just true about someone else.

Benchmarks are portraits. Insist on being the sitter.
