Halfway through this series, an honest recap of the single thesis underneath every post so far.

Identity is not a data problem. It is a claims-adjudication problem wearing a data costume. Every record is a claim that someone existed and acted. Every match is a judgment that two claims describe one person. Every threshold encodes how much evidence your organization requires before believing something about a human being.

Seen this way, the whole stack reorganizes. Normalization is evidence preparation. Matching is adjudication. The graph is case law, precedent that future decisions build on. Explainability is due process. Deletion is the right to have your case expunged, fully, including the precedents it touched.

Organizations that treat identity as plumbing get plumbing outcomes: leaks discovered by whoever lives downstream. Organizations that treat it as judgment build institutions: error rates, appeals, audits, accountability.

The second kind is rarer. It is also where I have decided to spend my career, and the next forty posts will stay on that ground.
