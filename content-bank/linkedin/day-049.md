Something underrated about working on identity inside a giant retailer: the scale breaks intuitions quietly, and the breakage is educational.

At small scale, edge cases are anecdotes. At hundreds of millions of profiles, a one-in-a-million edge case is a daily operational event with a queue. The customer with two hundred loyalty cards exists. The email address shared by an entire apartment building exists. Twins with identical names at the same address, both buying groceries, exist and confound every matcher ever written.

The discipline this forces is thinking in distributions rather than examples. No rule is evaluated on whether it handles the normal case. Every rule is evaluated on what its tail does at volume, because the tail arrives every morning.

This is also why I am skeptical of identity claims from anyone who has not operated at scale. The algorithms are public. The tail is proprietary, and the tail is the teacher.
