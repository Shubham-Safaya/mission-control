What frequency capping reveals about your identity graph, a diagnostic disguised as a feature.

Frequency capping sounds like the simplest promise in advertising: show this person the ad at most five times. Delivering it requires your identity system to recognize that person across every device, browser, and app where the campaign runs, in real time, without fail. It is a full-stack identity exam administered continuously.

Every false split becomes a violated cap: the person sees five impressions per fragment. Every false merge suppresses reach: two people share one cap. Panel studies keep finding real-world frequency wildly above declared caps for exactly this reason, and consumers experience it as the same ad, everywhere, forever.

If you want to audit a platform's identity quality without access to their graph, ask for the actual delivered frequency distribution against the cap. The variance is the graph's error, made visible.

Capping is where identity claims meet lived experience. The distribution never lies.
