Lessons from shipping an open-source identity resolution engine, part one: the algorithm was the easy month.

Jaro-Winkler similarity, blocking keys, transitive closure into connected components. Undergraduate material, honestly. What consumed the project was everything around it: normalization tables for nicknames, phone formats across countries, address standardization, and the API design question of how a caller expresses how sure they need to be.

The design choice I defend hardest: every merge is explainable. The engine returns which rule fired, which fields agreed, and the score. Not because it is elegant, but because I have sat in enterprise incident reviews where nobody could say why two customers became one, and the silence was expensive.

Explainability is not a feature you add to identity systems. It is the difference between a system and a liability.

The package is on PyPI. The downloads I am proudest of are the ones from university networks.
