Clean room adoption follows a pattern I now recognize: the first project is measurement theater, the third one is real.

Project one exists because two large partners need to announce a data collaboration. The overlap analysis runs, the number lands in a press release, and no operating decision changes. This is fine. It builds the pipes and teaches both legal teams to say yes.

Project two attempts activation and collides with the actual constraints: minimum cohort sizes, query rate limits, the discovery that the match key coverage between the two sides is thinner than either party advertised.

Project three, if the partnership survives, is the useful one. Scoped question, honest match expectations, output designed around aggregation limits rather than despite them, and someone on each side who now understands the other's identity spine.

If you are entering your first clean room deal, budget for this arc. The pipes are the deliverable. The insight arrives on the third try.
