Career note for PMs eyeing the identity and data platform space: the job is translation, and the accent matters.

In one week this role speaks to legal about deletion semantics, to data science about threshold calibration, to sales about why the match rate claim needs a footnote, to engineering about blocking key tradeoffs, and to a CFO about why maintaining an invisible graph deserves headcount.

None of these audiences shares vocabulary. All of them are right locally. The PM's actual output is a consistent identity policy that survives translation into five dialects, plus the judgment to know which precision claims each audience can absorb.

What prepared me was not analytics. It was writing. Forcing fuzzy matching decisions into plain sentences exposes exactly where the thinking is soft, and identity has infinite places for thinking to be soft.

If you want this specialization, start by explaining householding to someone who has never heard of it, in writing. That is the job.
