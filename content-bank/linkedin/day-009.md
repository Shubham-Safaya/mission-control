CDP architecture debates collapse into one question: where does identity resolution actually live?

Option one, the CDP does it. Convenient, vendor-supported, and you inherit whatever matching logic they built, at whatever precision they never quite publish.

Option two, resolution happens upstream in your warehouse, and the CDP consumes resolved profiles. More work, full control, and your match logic becomes something you can audit, version, and defend to a regulator.

Option three, the honest one for most enterprises: it is smeared across four systems and nobody can draw the diagram from memory. Every acquisition, every legacy loyalty platform, every regional stack adds another partial graph.

I have come to a firm view. Identity resolution is too consequential to be a feature of someone else's product. Own the logic, even if you rent the pipes. The vendors are good at pipes.

If you cannot reproduce why two records merged, you do not own your customer data. Your vendor does.
