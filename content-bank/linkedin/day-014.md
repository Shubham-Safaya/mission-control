CCPA in practice is nothing like CCPA in the compliance deck.

The deck version: consumers can request deletion, you delete, everyone moves on. The practice version: a deletion request arrives with one email address, and the identity graph knows that person as four emails, two phones, an address history, and device IDs braided into three downstream systems and a few vendor syncs.

Honoring that request correctly is an identity resolution problem before it is a legal one. Delete only the exact identifier submitted and you have technically responded while functionally retaining the person. Regulators have started noticing the difference.

This is why I tell privacy teams to sit inside the identity architecture reviews. The right to delete is really the right to be resolved and then deleted, and only the graph knows the full silhouette of a person.

Privacy engineering is identity engineering with a deadline. Teams that treat them as separate disciplines end up doing both badly.
