A confession about dashboards, from someone who has shipped too many.

Most identity dashboards I have built were designed to be looked at, not acted from. Match rate, seven-day trend, coverage by source. Handsome, informative, and decision-free: nothing on the screen told anyone what to do differently on Tuesday.

The redesign principle that fixed this: every tile must name its owner and its trigger. Merge-rate anomaly above threshold pages the identity on-call. Consent coverage below target opens a ticket against the loyalty team. Truth-set precision drift beyond budget freezes threshold changes until recalibration. The dashboard stops being a gallery and becomes a control panel.

The test is brutal and clarifying: for each metric, ask what would change if it doubled tomorrow. If the honest answer is someone would mention it in standup, delete the tile. It is decoration.

Attention is the scarcest resource in operations. Dashboards should spend it like money.
