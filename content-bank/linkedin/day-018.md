The unglamorous reason most CDP implementations disappoint: nobody agreed what a profile is before signing the contract.

Marketing wanted a person to message. Analytics wanted a consistent entity to count. Legal wanted a subject to delete. Loyalty wanted an account to credit. These sound compatible. They are four different resolution policies wearing one word.

A person-level profile for email frequency capping should not silently double as the household-level entity in your LTV model, and neither should be the unit you use to honor a deletion request. Precision requirements differ. Merge policies differ. Even the acceptable error direction differs: marketing tolerates false splits, measurement is poisoned by them.

The fix costs a whiteboard and an afternoon: write down each consumer of identity, its required entity, and its tolerance for each error type. Then configure resolution per consumer instead of pretending one setting serves all.

One graph, many views. Systems that skip this conversation have the conversation later, in an incident channel.
