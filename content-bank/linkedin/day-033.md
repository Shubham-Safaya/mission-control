The first-party data gold rush has a quiet inflation problem: everyone is counting the same customer three times.

A retailer says one hundred million profiles. Under it: loyalty accounts, app installs, and email subscribers, overlapping heavily, deduplicated optimistically or not at all. The bigger number wins the earnings call, so the bigger number ships.

I have started reading profile counts the way analysts read adjusted earnings. The questions that deflate honestly: unique resolved persons or raw accounts? Active on what recency window? What fraction carries a monetizable consent state and a reachable channel?

Inflated counts eventually get marked to market by advertisers, whose campaigns land on effective audiences a fraction of the promised size. Match rates become the polygraph.

If you run a data business, deduplicate before your buyers do it for you. The write-down is smaller when you take it yourself.
