Blocking keys are the most consequential performance decision in identity resolution, and almost nobody outside the team ever hears about them.

Naive matching compares every record with every other record. A million records means half a trillion comparisons. Nobody runs that. Instead you block: only compare records that share a coarse key, same postal code, same phonetic surname, same email domain and first initial.

Here is what makes it a product decision, not just an engineering one. Whatever your blocking key misses is unmatchable by construction. Block on surname phonetics and every marriage-related name change escapes comparison forever. Block on zip code and every mover splits into two people at the moment they move, which is precisely when retail wants them most.

When I review an identity system, I read the blocking strategy before the matching math, because the blocks define the ceiling. The matcher only decides how close you get to it.

The silent architecture decides. It usually decided years ago.
