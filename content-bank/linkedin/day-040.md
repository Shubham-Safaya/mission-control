I keep a private list of phrases that mean the identity conversation is about to get expensive. Sharing the top five.

Single customer view, when spoken by anyone who has not defined customer or view. Golden record, which implies a finished state that graphs do not have. We match on over two hundred signals, which is a complexity brag hiding a precision question. Privacy-safe, unaccompanied by a mechanism. And our graph is deterministic, from vendors whose coverage math only works if it is not.

None of these phrases is a lie exactly. They are anesthetics. Each one lets a room move past the hard question just before it gets asked.

The antidote costs one sentence: how do we know, at what error rate, measured against what. Ask it politely and watch which claims survive.

Vocabulary is cheap in this industry. Calibration is the whole game.
